Stage Fright is an up-and-coming alternative rock band. Lucy, Lily, Haley, Salemn, and Kaitlyn were once strangers but are now bound together by their unquenchable passion for new sound and music.
The story of Stage Fright began in 2020, the year of the pandemic.
Salemn, Lucy, and Kaitlyn were dedicated music students in need of a safe and available space to practice as the usual practice rooms reserved for music majors had been closed down. Quickly, an empty, run-down building at the corner of Bagby and Speight Avenue became prime real estate.
In the echoing, acoustic halls, Salemn, Lucy, and Kaitlyn began to pass by each other often, coming and going from their late-night practice sessions. But when their sounds overlapped, it soon became a fight to disrupt and overpower the others. Over time, the three were drawn to the ever-shifting sound of disequilibrium they generated together.
Salemn made the first move. Stopping the others one day in the same abandoned building, they asked them if they were willing to help them record their newest composition. Excited and willing, Kaitlyn began bringing her sister Lily, a keyboard hobbyist, to the practices; Salemn recruited Haley, a fellow music major, to play the drum kit.
Lucy's brutal electric guitar, Lily's classy keyboard, Haley's free-wheeling drumset, Salemn's metallic dry voice and Kaitlyn's steady bass layered onto each other to create new and passionate riffs.
In December 2020, the group made their official debut at the university's annual Winterfest. That night they exited the stage to the deafening cheers of their fellow students.


They named their group, Stage Fright, in an attempt to own their insecurities. What they did not know was how this would transform the meaning of the term 'stage fright'.
Janice Yoder (a member of the Rolling Stones) put it this way: "Stage fright is generally the nervousness one feels when on stage. With the lights blinding you and the crowd’s eyes fixed on your every move, it's hard not to be. But, Stage Fright, once on the stage, shows how to disown your fear...by owning it. Their music mesmerizes the fears of us all."
Stage Fright took their name to remind themselves of their insecurities that first day on stage. Despite their growth and gradual defeat of their fears, they hold onto the name as a reminder of how far they’ve come.
The band has released two albums and one single thus far, and more music is coming soon!

2020

On with the show!

In September, the group meets each other during a talent show hosted by their university. In November, the five officially banded together and did their first small concert for their university in December. They also earn their name Stage Fright from a colleague.

2021

The Music Era Begins

In early February, Stage Fright released their first album called “Tremble”. The band also released their first single “Subdue” in late August. More importantly, Stage Fright did their first tour around Texas from June to August. They played for their university as guests for the annual festival of music and art.

2022

Accompany Fear Itself

Stage Fright released their second single “Dear Phobos” to start the year off. Three of the members also graduated with their Bachelor degrees tucked underneath their arms. The band did their second Texan tour in the summertime. A second album “Lost in the Crowd” is released in August and a single “Glossophobia” is released to end the year.

2023

Our Build in Music

“Surmount” the album is released in March. They did their annual (third) Texan Music Tour from June to August. They released one single and one album by the end of the year. In July, “Hysteria”, the single, was given out to the public. In October, the album “Beginner's Luck” was released.

2024

A Foot Across the Line

The single “Butterflies” gets released in late January. Lily and Kaitlyn graduated with their Bachelor degrees in their back pocket, and Haley and Lucy graduated with their Master degree. The band did their first tour out of Texas, traveling to the states New Mexico, Colorado, and Oklahoma for four months. Stage Fright released the album “Conquer” in the middle of the Out-of-State Tour in July. They released their most recent album “Exit Right” in October.

The Future Awaits
